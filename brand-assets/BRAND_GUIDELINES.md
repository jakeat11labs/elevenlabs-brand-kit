## 1. Logotype

### Logo Design
- "Our logo captures the essence of ElevenLabs and unites everything we do. Its powerfully simple design ensures we stand out in a crowded space."
- Described as: **Modular, Sharp & Precise**

### Safespace
- All communications must adhere to a minimum area of clearance equal to the logo's height on all sides.

### Symbol (II Icon)
- "The visual shorthand for our brand is our 11 icon, constructed from two distinctively simple vertical lines."
- "It abstracts the number eleven from our name, while also subtly referencing the pause icon seen when listening to audio."
- Sizes: Large (80px height), Medium (40px), Small (24px)

### Logo Don'ts
- Don't stack the wordmark or alter the 11 icon
- Don't use a shorthand in any logo variants
- Don't apply effects like drop shadows
- Don't apply strokes or outlines
- Don't rotate or angle the logo
- Don't apply color to the logo
- Don't stretch or deform the logo
- Don't recreate the logo in other fonts

### Wordmark Text Don'ts
- No space between Eleven and Labs (not "Eleven Labs")
- Always capitalize the L (not "Elevenlabs")
- Always capitalize the E (not "elevenlabs")
- Never all caps (not "ELEVENLABS")
- Don't use capitalized I's to recreate the 11 icon (not "IIElevenLabs")
- Don't use 11 to recreate the 11 icon (not "11ElevenLabs")

## 2. Typography

### Brand Font: KMR Waldenburg
- "Typography is set exclusively in KMR Waldenburg, used in two weights to create a clear and cohesive visual hierarchy."
- "The typeface blends humanist and organic elements. Its precise construction is characterized by low stroke contrast and prominent vertical lines."
- **Two weights only:** Normal (bold/confident) and Book (refined/light)

### Typographic Balance
- "It's not only about hierarchy but how text elements relate. Weight, size, line height, and tracking combine to create rhythm, contrast, and harmony across formats."

| Use | Font | Size | Line-height | Tracking |
|-----|------|------|-------------|----------|
| Large headline | KMR Book | 80px | 100% | -3% |
| Medium headline | KMR Normal | 58px | 100% | -2% |
| Body/subhead | KMR Book | 27px | 120% | -1% |

## 3. Color Palette

### Primary Colors
- "The primary palette is minimal and monochrome, forming a clean foundation."
- "Color comes mainly from images or video, treated with effects to create a bright, saturated, and positive atmosphere, with warm hues balanced by vibrant blues."

| Name | Hex | RGB |
|------|-----|-----|
| **Graphite** | #1E1916 | R30 G25 B22 |
| **Off-White** | #FAFAFA | R250 G250 B250 |
| **Cream** | #F5F3F1 | R245 G242 B241 |
| **Black** | #000000 | R0 G0 B0 |

- Color is **image-based** — it comes from imagery and backgrounds, not from separate color palettes.

### Color Proportion
- "The overall layout should remain mostly clean and monochrome, with color accents making up a smaller portion of the visual space."
- "Color or media can fill small backgrounds or occasionally expand into larger sections."
- **Gray shades should not be used with color accents.**

### Color Application Modes
1. **Core Brand** (general applications) — New features, product releases, partnerships. Uses full brand palette with imagery.
2. **Monochrome** (technical applications) — Developers, case studies, product insights, guides. Uses only black, white, and gray with optional third-party imagery.

### Monochrome Mode
- "Used for developer-related content such as documentation, product guides, or minimal layouts."
- "These rely only on black, white, and gray, without core brand imagery. Third-party photos, logos, or assets may be included when relevant."

## 4. Image Use

### Philosophy
- "Images bring in abstract color, treated with grain and blur so they dissolve into tone, light and texture, emphasizing atmosphere rather than literal content."
- "Even if not fully visible, imagery should follow a sound metaphor."
- "Through masks or patterns, it should still evoke a sound environment, helping users see what they might otherwise hear."

### Image Selection
- Use royalty-free or AI-generated images with a bright, saturated palette.
- "Motion blur or movement helps convey the desired sound atmosphere."
- "Images should feel saturated, dreamlike and imaginative, pointing to a hopeful, sensory future."

### Image Generation (AI)
- "A reference image can define palette or setting. Prompts should capture the atmosphere, with camera styles or framing added if needed."
- "Keep images evocative and atmospheric, considering later edits like blur or overlays."
- Use moodboards or reference links in prompts to guide palette and atmosphere.
- "Keep prompts short and focused on mood or movement, adding styles only when needed."

### Noise Treatment
- "Apply a subtle noise effect to all assets."
- "Ensure it is delicate and does not alter saturation, luminosity or quality."
- **Settings:** 70% opacity, Overlay blend mode

### Simple Blur
- "Apply a subtle blur to images or video."
- "This softens shapes and creates warmth and atmosphere, though values may vary by image size."
- **Typical value:** 25

### Complex Blur
- "Use two blur layers instead of blurring the base image."
- "First: heavy blur over the composition. Second: lighter blur with shape or pattern revealing parts below."
- "The base image remains unedited."
- **Settings:** Layer 1 blur: 180, Layer 2 opacity: 0.1

### Blur Framing
- "Avoid tight crops that turn images into color patches, keep some structure."
- "Do not mix too many colors in one crop to prevent visual noise."

### Blur Amount
- "Adjust blur per composition, for the whole image or sections."
- "Do not over-blur to muddy colors."
- "With complex blur, keep composition clean and ensure text or logos remain legible."

## 5. Graphics — Chladni Patterns & Shapes

### Chladni Patterns
- "Chladni patterns emerge when sound vibrations form visible shapes, turning sound into something we can see."
- "They highlight our deep connection to voice and audio and are a core element of ElevenLabs' visual language."

### Usage
- "Chladni patterns can appear in monochrome or on colored backgrounds."
- "They may also be layered over imagery and combined with subtle noise textures for added depth."

### Chladni Shapes
- "Chladni shapes are another way to visualize sound."
- "They expand the expressive potential of our system and remain an important element of our graphics."

### Shapes as Masks
- "These shapes are most often used in combination with photography, serving as masks that overlay images to create distinctive compositions."

### Clarity and Focus
- "An example of treatment is sharp Chladni shapes over blurred imagery."
- "This contrast emphasizes focus, clarity, and the precision of our technology."

### Patterns + Color
- Chladni patterns and shapes combine with the color palette to enrich communication and create dynamic visuals.

### Monochrome Applications
- "Chladni patterns in monochrome are mostly used for developer-related content."
- "Opacity and stroke weight should be adjusted to keep them subtle and sophisticated yet visible across different formats."

### Linear Illustrations
- "Linear illustrations are another part of our visual language."
- "They bring structure and highlight our research-driven perspective."
- Can be applied both in monochrome and in combination with color.

### Creating Patterns
- Patterns can be generated from scratch using dedicated tools.
- Open the SVG in Illustrator to refine node structure.
- Use the Simplify function or manually tweak paths for the final result.

## Key Brand Rules Summary

1. **Font:** KMR Waldenburg only. Two weights: Normal and Book.
2. **Colors:** Monochrome foundation (Graphite, Off-White, Cream, Black). Color comes from imagery, not palettes.
3. **No gray with color accents.**
4. **Images:** Saturated, dreamlike, motion-blurred. Sound metaphor. Always treated with noise (70% overlay) and blur.
5. **Chladni patterns:** Core brand element. Used as overlays, masks, and standalone graphics.
6. **Logo:** Never alter, never color, never rotate. Safespace = logo height.
7. **Wordmark:** Always "ElevenLabs" — one word, capital E and L. Never recreate the II icon with text characters.
8. **Two modes:** Core Brand (colorful imagery) for general use. Monochrome (black/white/gray only) for developer/technical content.
9. **Blur treatment:** Simple blur (25) or Complex blur (180 heavy + 0.1 reveal layer). Base image stays unedited.
10. **Noise:** Always present. Subtle. 70% opacity, overlay blend mode.
